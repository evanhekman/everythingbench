# AGENT INSTRUCTIONS
Here is an example of how you will interact with the game. You will be provided with sections of a log documenting the game, and will be asked to make at least one decision each turn. If your action requires supporting actions (i.e. buying resources from a neighbor), you should specify them before the action. If the action is automatic (i.e. a card is built for free) you do not need to specify it. If you attempt to perform an invalid action, you will receive an error from the engine and will be asked to try again.

This is an example of the log format you will receive (plain text, easy to read and scan). Within a round you only see your own private decision (other players' simultaneous choices are hidden until the round results).

```
=== AGE 1 ===
--- Round 1 ---
--- Your Turn ---
hand: [baths, scriptorium, timber_yard, marketplace, ...]
coins: 3
wonder_stages_built: 0
your_production: []
left (Player 0) production: []
right (Player 2) production: []

(If this is a retry after an invalid move, the error + updated neighbor production will be inserted here.)

(Here you will be asked to select a move)
Example: play marketplace

Round 1 Summary:
Player 0 played ore_vein
Player 1 (you) played marketplace
Player 2 played glassworks

--- Round 2 ---
--- Your Turn ---
hand: [apothecary, workshop, ...]
coins: 3
wonder_stages_built: 0
your_production: []
left (Player 0) production: [ore]
right (Player 2) production: [glass]

(Here you will be asked to select a move)
Example: play apothecary right:glass

Round 2 Summary:
Player 0 played brickyard
Player 1 (you) bought glass from player 2
Player 1 (you) played apothecary
Player 2 played excavation

--- Round 3 ---
--- Your Turn ---
hand: [scriptorium, east_trading_post, ...]
coins: 2
wonder_stages_built: 0
your_production: []
left (Player 0) production: [ore, brick]
right (Player 2) production: [glass, ore/brick]

Please select your action for this round (play, burn, wonder):
Example: play east_trading_post

Round 3 Summary:
Player 0 played loom
Player 1 (you) played east_trading_post
Player 2 played west_trading_post

...

--- Age 1 Summary ---
Player 0 built 0 wonder stages
Player 1 (you) built 0 wonder stages
Player 2 built 0 wonder stages

Player 0 gets +1, -1 from battles
Player 1 (you) gets +1, +1 from battles
Player 2 gets -1, -1 from battles

=== AGE 2 ===
...

```

The log only shows completed previous rounds + your current private decision block. You never see simultaneous decisions other players are making in the current round.

Battles are resolved only at the end of each full age (after round 6), not after individual rounds.

When it is your turn, the log will end with your open decision block (starting with "--- Player X decision ---"). That block contains your private state. Output exactly one line with your action.

Error messages are injected directly into the decision block (e.g. "ERROR: Insufficient stone. Neighbors currently produce: ..."). Read them and correct your next output. You get up to 3 attempts per decision.

An error message might look something like this:
```
ERROR: Insufficient resources to perform this action. Please select a different action or specify trades using left:resource / right:resource.
```

YOUR AVAILABLE ACTIONS:
play <card_id> [left:<resource> right:<resource> ...]
wonder <card_id> [left:<resource> right:<resource> ...]
burn <card_id>

Use the dir:resource notation for trades (only when needed). Repeat the token for multiples (e.g. left:stone left:stone). You can only buy resources your neighbors are producing. Example:
```
play baths left:stone
```

A more complex example:
```
play walls left:stone left:stone right:ore
```

To build a wonder stage, pay the cost of the wonder instead of the cost of your card and select any card to bury underneath the wonder slot. Since stages must be built sequentially, there is only one possible next stage. For example, this move would bury the altar under your next available wonder slot.
```
wonder altar
```

If you need coins, you may burn a card for +3 coins. Example:
```
burn pantheon
```

You must make an immediate decision using the information in the log. Do not ask questions. The engine will tell you (via error text in place of your next decision block) if the action was invalid. If you attempt 3 invalid actions in a row, you will forfeit the game.
